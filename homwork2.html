“AI slop” the article by Nesrine Malik argues that Al-generated content is changing how people see and react to reality. Malik’s main point is not just that fake images exist, but that so many images real and fake mixed together make it harder for people to care or respond seriously. AI blurs the line between real and fake when real news for example like war or any news related to politics appears next to an AI made images or videos everything just starts to feel the same. People then start to become unsure of what to trust and Malik goes further she says that the bigger problem is that people stop reacting strongly at all. Even real suffering can feel distant or unreal because it’s just another thing in the feed. I would also like to say that I agree with what Malik says next and I am about to talk about it that AI contents often push certain ideas especially nostalgic or conservative ones some AI images show “perfect” families or traditional gender roles. Malik says that this happens because AI is trained on past data which contains bias. Me myself I have been a victim of looking at a picture and thinking that it was a real-life picture but no it was completely AI generated or made using AI. Day by day AI is getting more scarier and out of control now sometimes looking at videos and images online I wonder to myself if this is AI generated or a real one. Social media platforms have encouraged the use of AI a lot. Companies like Facebook promotes content that gets attention even if it’s a low quality or misleading. AI slop is cheap and very easy to produce so it spreads quickly. The goal isn’t the truth it’s about the engagement. This means only one thing that is to keep users constantly fed on content designed to keep them scrolling and not thinking. Ai also spreads through private spaces like what’s app when fake images are being shared by friends and family people are more likely to trust them blindly. This makes the problem worse because it feels personal and believable. But overall, Malik’s main argument is that AI slop doesn’t just trick people it also overwhelms them. When everything feels like content, even serious events start losing their impact this leads to a kind of numbness where people know bad things are happening but don’t feel urgency to act. That’s what she means by when she says, “sleepwalking into disaster”.
